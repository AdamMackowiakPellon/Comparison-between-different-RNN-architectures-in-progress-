# Comparison-between-different-RNN-architectures-in-progress-
A small project were I compare the performance of different recurrent neural networks, such as vanilla RNN, LSTM, GRU with Echo State Networks and Delay Reservoir computing (the ones were I'm doing research right now). In order to make the comparison more fair I'm Optuna for the hyperparameter optimization.

This project has been originated mainly from personal motivation in order to understand what I am doing what I am doing — To understand better why I am currently doing research in delay reservoir computing. I know for a fact that this field is not a waste of time, given that, first, it's a way to solve the vanishing gradient problem that takes place in vanilla RNN, but also because, contrary to more conventional architectures, ESN (Echo State Networks) and by extension TDRC (Time-Delay Reservoir Computing), can be implemented using hardware, as electric circuits or, more interestingly, lasers, given that the information processing speed is a substantial improvement since, in that case, you have light instead of currents of electrons. It could be an interesting topic to explore on a paper but, fortunately or unfortunately, it has been already cover in the paper [Backpropagation Algorithms and Reservoir Computing in Recurrent Neural Networks for the Forecasting of Complex Spatiotemporal Dynamics](https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.05266). There are some things that they haven't looked at (for example, they use conventional ESNs and there is no look at TDRC) and also they have not used hyperparameter optimization algorithms like, for example, TPESampler, which unfortunately adds biases in their study. Nevertheless, it's a very complete and in-depth look at the topic and I suggest everybody that has interest on it to read it.

As I am doing this project I am learning more and more, so any questions or suggestions are more than welcome.
